The most popular trekking trail in Nepal escaped damage during April's devastating earthquake and is safe for hikers to return, an assessment team said Thursday.
Tulsi Prasad Gautam of Nepal's Tourism Department said the assessment would help bring back trekkers, and it would be easier for them to get travel insurance - a major concern for people planning their trip to Nepal.
The trail around Mount Annapurna gets about 115,000 foreign trekkers, which is about 60 per cent of all hikers who travel to Nepal every year.
