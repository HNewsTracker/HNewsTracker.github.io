DUESSELDORF, Germany (AP) - The coffins of 15 of the 16 German high school students killed in the Germanwings plane crash were leaving Duesseldorf airport on Wednesday in a hearse convoy to their hometown of Haltern.
A spokeswoman for Germanwings said the remains of the other victims to their home countries had also begun and would likely be finished by the end of June.
