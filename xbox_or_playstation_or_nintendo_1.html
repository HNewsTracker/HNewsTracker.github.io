Nintendo CEO Satoru Iwata dies
TOKYO (Reuters) - Japanese videogame maker Nintendo Co Ltd's chief executive and president, Satoru Iwata, died on Saturday due to a growth in his bile duct, the company said.
Iwata had been a director at Kyoto-based Nintendo since 2000, and was appointed president in 2002.
Presiding over a news conference in May to announce Nintendo's full-year financial results, Iwata had said the company's long-awaited entry into smartphone games would help it double annual operating profit in the year to next March.
