Apple has cash cow in iPhone even as phone industry slows
Several analysts say their studies confirm Apple CEO Tim Cook's claim that many recent iPhone buyers are people who switched from Android phones.
Apple likely sold about 47 million iPhones during the most recent quarter, or 34 percent more than a year earlier, according to analysts polled by FactSet.
Most experts say iPhone sales will get another bump when Apple releases new models this fall.

Apple also forecast that revenue for the quarter ending in September will fall between $49 billion and $51 billion US, indicating total sales could fall below Wall Street estimates of $50.8 billion US.
Apple has previously said it sold 1 million iPhones in the first 74 days, or more than 10 weeks, after sales began in 2007.
For the latest quarter, Apple said revenue from all sources grew 33 per cent from last year to $49.6 billion US in the April-June quarter, with the iPhone contributing $31.4 billion US in sales.
That beat the estimates of Wall Street analysts surveyed by FactSet, who were expecting Apple to report earnings of $1.81 per share on sales of $49.25 billion US.
